The Regime Classifier is the first layer of Aerondight's scoring pipeline. It answers a simple but critical question: **what kind of market are we in right now?**

## Four Distinct States

The system classifies the market into one of four regimes:

| Regime | Character | Typical VIX |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **Bull Broad** | Real economy rally, broad participation | Low |
| **Bull Tech** | Tech-led narrow rally, weak breadth | Low |
| **Correction** | Grinding bear, defensive outperformance | ~24 |
| **Crisis** | Sharp crash, correlations spike | ~45 |

## Dual-Model Validation

A **Hidden Markov Model** identifies regime clusters from historical macro data — it finds patterns without being told what to look for. An independent **XGBoost classifier** validates the HMM's output using a broader feature set.

When both models agree, signal confidence increases. When they disagree, the system becomes more conservative.

## Impact on Scoring

The regime classification feeds directly into the stock scoring engine. The same stock can receive different scores depending on the current market regime. This context-awareness is what separates Aerondight from static factor models.

![Regime timeline showing SPY price with color-coded backgrounds](/regime/regime_timeline.png)

Crisis regimes are rare (~6% of historical time) but are where the long-term model finds its best opportunities — patient capital deployed during panic historically produces outsized returns.
